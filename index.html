Overview

Solána Imani Rowe (born November 8, 1989) known professionally as SZA, is an American R&B singer-songwriter.
She was born in St. Louis, Missouri, and raised in Maplewood, New Jersey.

Career

When she started her music career, she made two EPs. After that, she signed with the hip hop record label Top Dawg Entertainment.
Through the signing, SZA released her third studio EP, Z, in 2014.
During that same year, she co-wrote "Feeling Myself" with Nicki Minaj and Beyoncé.
In 2016, she was featured in Rihanna's song "Consideration".In 2017, she released her first studio album, Ctrl.
In 2020, she released the single "Good Days". In 2021, she released the single "I Hate U"
and collaborated with Doja Cat to make the song "Kiss Me More".
In 2022, SZA released her second studio album, "SOS".

Awards/Nominations

Sza has had a total of 27 awards and 69 Nominations include

  • BET Awards for Best New Artist - 2018
  • BET Award for Best Female R&B/Pop Artist - 2023
  • MTV Video Music Award for Best Collaboration - 2021
  • iHeartRadio Music Award for R&B Song of the Year - 2023
